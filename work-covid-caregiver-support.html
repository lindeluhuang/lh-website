COVID-19 Caregiver Support

Problem

It is often the informal caregivers (the spouse, the parent, the child), who bears the burden of taking care of the patient before and after their hospital visit. However, these caregivers are often neglected by the hospital system and offered little to no guidance or support.

The informal caregiver neglect is made worse with COVID-19 when strict isolation policies make the caregiver unable to be at the patient bedside. When a caregiver is fortunate enough to see their loved one come home from the hospital, they are often given insufficient instructions on how to provide care at home. This can cause mismanagement of care, leading to unnecessarily agonizing or incomplete recovery.

Proposal

We want to provide a system of support for the informal caregivers through all stages of the COVID journey, particularly focusing on recovering from the hospital stay. We are working with systems in the medical center to ensure that it is not just a toolkit in a silo, but rather an entire ecosystem of service that offers the guidance the caregivers deserve.

High-level Plan

  1. Conduct interviews with patients, caregivers, and groups of people closely related to the patients and caregivers
  2. Synthesize research findings
  3. Design initial prototype
  4. Create experiment canvas and methodically iterate over multiple prototypes
  5. Release the solution throughout the University of Michigan hospital and study the impact on outcomes

Partners

We're working on this project in partnership with the University of Michigan’s Institute for Social Research.
